# Evidence protocol

The single rule that keeps this engine honest. A smart founder's main failure mode is
reasoning to confident conclusions from inside their own head. First-principles decomposition
makes this worse, because decomposition generates reasons to believe. This protocol forces
every conclusion to earn its confidence from contact with the market.

## The five tiers of evidence

- **Tier 0, assumption.** A belief in your head. No external support.
- **Tier 1, signal.** Indirect support: analogous data, a competitor's behaviour, search
  volume, a forum thread. Suggestive, not confirming.
- **Tier 2, conversation.** A real prospect in your actual segment said it, in their words,
  unprompted by your pitch.
- **Tier 3, paid commitment.** Someone put something at stake: a deposit, a pre-order, a
  signed LOI, a paying invoice, a booked paid pilot.
- **Tier 4, repeatable.** Tier 3 happened at least 3 times through the same motion, so it is
  a pattern, not luck.

## The confidence gate

- Any value-equation score, base rate, risk ranking, or strategic conclusion that rests on
  tier 0 or tier 1 evidence is **capped at LOW confidence**.
- When a conclusion is capped at LOW, the output is **not a strategy**. The output is the
  cheapest action that moves it up a tier. You do not get to decide direction on a guess; you
  get to go find out.
- A conclusion may be stated with MEDIUM or HIGH confidence only when it rests on tier 2 or
  higher, and you name the specific evidence.

## The hard override

Zero buyer conversations in the last 30 days overrides every other recommendation the engine
might produce. The next move is a 5-conversation sprint with real prospects. No amount of
modelling substitutes for this. A pre-revenue founder who keeps receiving "homework" instead
of "go talk to buyers" is being failed by the tool.

## Running the 5-conversation sprint (the Mom Test)

A founder who has never done discovery will botch the conversations by pitching. The rules:
ask about their life and what they did the last time they had the problem, never about your
idea; do not pitch, do not lead, do not ask "would you use this," ask "what do you use now and
what did it cost you." Good signal is a past action or a payment, not a compliment. End each
conversation knowing one concrete thing the person already did about the problem.

## What counts as buyer evidence

Buyer evidence is what a specific, real, in-segment prospect did or said, captured verbatim
or as an artifact. It is not: your own logic, a friend's encouragement, a "this would be
useful" with no commitment, a large market size, or a competitor's existence. Capture the
quote, the date, and who said it, into the record's assumptions block.
