# Template: Product/Tool Announcement (Short Form / X)

> Template for announcing a new product or tool on X or equivalent short form platforms. Used as a standalone post or as a quote post embedding the company's official release announcement. Referenced by the **tool-announce** workflow.
>
> Voice: [[voice/brand-voice]]
> Audience: [[audience/]] (use the audience profile for your short form platform)
> Platform: [[platforms/x-post]] or equivalent short form platform

---

## Post Structure

```
[1 to 2 sentence hook: what this tool does or why it matters]

[2 to 3 sentences: your take on it, how it fits in the ecosystem, what makes it interesting or what you are skeptical about]

[Optional: one specific capability that stands out, described concretely]

Full breakdown: {{link to your review or the product page}}

[QUOTE POST (optional): embed the company's official release post, which displays as a card below your text]
```

**Format note:** Extended post length is available on many platforms for verified accounts. No need to thread; the entire post fits in a single post in most cases.

---

## Hook Formulas

Choose the one that fits the product best.

### 1. Curiosity hook
Lead with an unexpected angle or counterintuitive framing.

> "{{Company}} just shipped {{tool name}} and the most interesting part is not the headline feature."

> "Everyone is talking about {{tool name}}'s {{obvious feature}}. The part worth paying attention to is {{less obvious thing}}."

### 2. Capability hook
Lead with what the tool concretely enables.

> "{{Tool name}} does {{specific thing}} in {{specific time/way}}. That is a meaningful shift for {{who benefits}}."

> "You can now {{concrete capability}} without {{previous friction}}. {{Tool name}} just launched."

### 3. Ecosystem hook
Position the tool within the broader landscape.

> "{{Category}} just got more interesting. {{Tool name}} from {{company}} sits between {{tool A}} and {{tool B}} in a way that none of the existing options cover."

> "If you are using {{related tool}}, {{new tool}} is worth watching. It fills the {{specific gap}} that {{related tool}} does not address."

---

## Body: Your Take

This is where the post earns its value. Do not just summarize the announcement; add perspective.

**What to include (pick 2 to 3):**
- Where this fits in the ecosystem and what it competes with
- What makes it genuinely different from alternatives (be specific)
- What you are skeptical about or what remains unproven
- A concrete use case that demonstrates the capability
- How it changes a workflow for a specific type of user
- What the pricing means relative to alternatives

**How to write it:**
- First person when you have used it or have an informed opinion ("I have been testing..." or "Based on the architecture...")
- Direct assessment, not hedging ("This is useful for X" rather than "This could potentially be useful")
- Name competing tools when the comparison matters
- One sharp point is better than three soft ones

---

## The Standout Detail (Optional)

If one specific capability is genuinely noteworthy, call it out separately. This works best when the detail is concrete and surprising.

> "The part that caught my attention: {{specific capability}} works with {{specific context}}, which means {{practical implication}}."

Only include this if it adds something the hook and body did not already cover. If the post is already strong without it, leave it out.

---

## Link and Quote Post

End with a link to your review or breakdown page, then optionally embed the company's official post as a quote post.

```
Full breakdown: {{link-to-your-review-or-product-page}}
```

The quote post (the company's official announcement) provides visual credibility and links back to the source. Find the official post on the platform before publishing.

---

## Voice Notes

**Sound like:** Someone who tracks this space seriously and shares an informed take. Direct, technical when the detail matters, slightly skeptical of marketing claims, specific about what is actually useful.

**What to avoid:**
- Hype words: "game changer," "revolutionary," "insane," "wild," "crazy," "massive"
- Superlatives: "the best," "the only," "the ultimate"
- FOMO: "you're falling behind," "everyone is switching to this"
- Filler enthusiasm: "This is huge!" or "I'm so excited about this"
- Vague praise without substance: "This looks really promising" (promising for what?)
- Announcing features you have not verified yourself; be honest about what you know vs. what the marketing claims

**Do use:**
- Tool names when the comparison matters
- Concrete details: numbers, speeds, pricing, specific capabilities
- Honest qualifiers: "I have not tested this yet but," "based on the announcement," "if this works as described"
- First person singular
- Short, direct sentences

See [[voice/brand-voice]] for your complete voice guidelines.

## Update Variant

When announcing a **product update** (not a new product), the framing shifts from introducing the product to highlighting what changed and why it matters.

### Post Structure (Update)

```
[1 to 2 sentence hook: what changed and why it matters]

[2 to 3 sentences: what the update actually adds, with specific capabilities. Compare old vs new where the contrast is meaningful.]

[Optional: one standout detail from the update]

Updated breakdown: {{link to your review or product page}}

[QUOTE POST (optional): embed the company's update announcement, if available]
```

### Hook Formulas (Update)

#### 1. Before/after hook
Lead with the contrast between the old and new version.

> "{{Tool name}} went from {{old description}} to {{new description}}. That is not an incremental update."

> "{{Tool name}} used to {{old limitation}}. The new version {{new capability}}."

#### 2. Specific improvement hook
Lead with the single most impressive change.

> "{{Tool name}} just shipped {{specific new feature}} and it changes how {{specific workflow}} works."

> "The new {{tool name}} update: {{specific capability}} that was previously impossible."

#### 3. Reframing hook
The update changes what category the tool belongs in.

> "{{Tool name}} used to be a {{old category}}. After this update, it is a {{new category}}."

### Body: What Actually Changed

Focus on the delta, not the full feature list. The reader may or may not know the tool already, so briefly orient them, but spend most of the post on what is new.

**What to include (pick 2 to 3):**
- The most significant new capability and what it enables
- What the tool could not do before that it can do now
- How this changes the tool's competitive position
- Pricing changes, if any
- Who benefits most from this specific update

**What to avoid:**
- Repeating the company's full feature list as if every item is equally important
- Treating incremental improvements as transformative
- Covering every small change; pick the 2 to 3 that actually matter

### Example (Update)

```
Google Stitch went from a text to UI generator to a full design agent with an infinite canvas, voice mode, and React app export. That is a different tool than what launched in January.

The design agent reasons across your entire project history and runs parallel explorations. DESIGN.md lets you extract a portable design system from any URL and carry it between projects. The output is now functional React with Tailwind, not static HTML mockups.

Daily credits replaced monthly limits. The SDK is open source with 2.4k GitHub stars and a growing skills ecosystem.

Updated breakdown: https://yourbrand.com/tools/stitch

[QUOTE POST: @GoogleLabs' Stitch update announcement]
```
