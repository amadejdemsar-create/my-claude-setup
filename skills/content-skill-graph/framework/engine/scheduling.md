# Weekly Posting Schedule

> The default weekly rhythm for content across your active platforms. Each slot is marked as either a big swing (20% of posts, full creative energy) or fast/volume (80% of posts, under 15 minutes). This is a guideline, not a rigid calendar. Real events and product launches override the schedule.

References: [[engine/power-law]], [[engine/hit-detection]], [[engine/content-repurposing]], [[engine/repurpose-rules]], [[config]]

---

## How to Use This Schedule

This schedule is a template. Adapt it to your platforms (configured in [[config]]), your audience (defined in [[audience/]]), and your capacity. The principles matter more than the specific day assignments:

- **Two big swing days per week** (default: Monday and Sunday). These get real creative energy.
- **Fast posts fill the remaining days.** Each one takes under 15 minutes.
- **Daily engagement work** (comments, replies) is as important as posting.
- **One batch session per week** (default: Sunday) to prep the week ahead.

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday (Big Swing Day)

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Primary platform | **BIG SWING** | First principles take from something you experienced at work. This gets the best hook, the sharpest thinking, and a visual if possible. |
| Secondary platform | **BIG SWING** | Same underlying idea, rewritten for the secondary audience. Not a translation; a separate post built from the same raw insight. Must land on what that audience cares about. |

Monday sets the tone for the week. Both posts get real creative energy. If you only have time for one big swing this week, make it Monday.

### Tuesday

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Primary platform | **FAST** | Content asset spotlight, build-in-public note, or workflow observation. Use existing content assets (see [[engine/content-repurposing]]) when possible. |
| Both | Engagement | Reply to comments on Monday's posts. Add 3 to 5 useful replies on other people's posts. |

### Wednesday

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Primary platform | **FAST** | Comparison post, opinion piece, or industry angle. |
| N/A | Prep | Collect raw notes for Friday and Sunday posts. |

### Thursday

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Secondary platform | **FAST** | Practical business example, before and after story, or market observation. Pull from existing content assets whenever a relevant angle fits. |
| Both | Engagement | Do comment work, not just posting. |

### Friday

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Primary platform | **FAST** | Proof post with a number, a friction point, or a tiny case. |
| Primary platform | **FAST** (optional) | Second lighter post if the week produced something worth showing. |

### Saturday

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Primary platform | **FAST** | Rough reflection, screenshot note, or mini lesson from the week. |
| Secondary platform | Skip | No need to force a post unless there is a genuinely strong angle. |

### Sunday (Big Swing Day)

| Platform | Type | What to post |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Secondary platform | **BIG SWING** | Weekly reflection, what changed, what became simpler, or what your audience keeps getting wrong. Give this one real effort. |
| N/A | Batch | Draft next week's posts for 60 to 90 minutes. |

## Weekly Totals

| Platform | Posts per week | Big swings | Fast posts |
|----------|---------------|------------|------------|
| Primary | 6 | 1 (Monday) | 5 (Tue, Wed, Fri x2, Sat) |
| Secondary | 4 | 2 (Monday, Sunday) | 2 (Thursday, plus flexible slot) |
| Engagement | Daily | N/A | 15 to 30 minutes |

Adjust these totals based on your capacity. The minimum viable volume for the power law to work is six to ten posts per week across platforms. See [[engine/power-law]].

## Repurposing Rule

Do not translate posts line by line between platforms. Take one raw idea and write it twice:

- **Builder/technical platform version:** Can stay narrower and more technical. Name specific tools, steps, and numbers. The audience already works with these tools daily; they want leverage, not motivation.
- **Business/professional platform version:** Must land on time, cost, clarity, or workflow. Explain the business consequence, not the technical mechanism. Use natural language appropriate to your audience.

Each post must work for someone who will never see the other version. See [[engine/repurpose-rules]] for the full protocol.

## When to Override the Schedule

The schedule is a default, not a commitment. Override it when:

- **A product, tool, or industry development launches** that is relevant to your audience. Speed to market matters more than following the plan. Post a hot take or first impression the same day.
- **A hit happens** and compounding is more valuable than the next scheduled post. See [[engine/hit-detection]].
- **Real work produces a genuinely good story** mid-week. A Thursday build-in-public post that is better than anything on the schedule should replace whatever was planned.
- **Nothing worth posting exists** for a particular slot. Skipping is better than posting filler. A zero-value post trains the audience to scroll past your content.

## Weekly Batch Session

The weekly batch session (default: Sunday, 60 to 90 minutes) is the engine that makes the rest of the week sustainable.

**What to do in the batch:**
1. Review the week's engagement numbers. Update rolling averages. Identify any hits that need compounding (see [[engine/hit-detection]]).
2. Pick the Monday big swing topic. Write the first draft for both platform versions.
3. Scan your existing content assets for 2 to 3 spotlight, comparison, or roundup candidates for the week's fast posts. See [[engine/content-repurposing]].
4. Draft 2 to 3 fast posts. They do not need to be final; rough drafts that can be polished in 5 minutes on the day are fine.
5. Note any topics, reactions, or observations from the week that could fuel future big swings.

**What not to do in the batch:**
- Do not try to write all posts in final form. The fast posts should stay fast, even during batch prep.
- Do not plan more than one week ahead. The content landscape changes too quickly, and over-planning leads to stale posts.
- Do not skip the engagement review. The numbers from the past week directly inform what to write next week.
