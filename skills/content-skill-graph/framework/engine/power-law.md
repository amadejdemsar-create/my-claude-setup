# Power Law Content Strategy

> Content follows a power law: a small number of posts generate the vast majority of reach, followers, and inbound conversations. Everything else is a lottery ticket. This file defines how to allocate creative energy across posts to maximize the chance of outlier performance.

References: [[engine/hit-detection]], [[engine/content-repurposing]], [[engine/scheduling]]

---

## The Core Insight

Most content advice optimizes for consistency and average performance. That misses the point. The distribution of content outcomes is not normal; it follows a power law. One post that hits can outperform an entire month of average posts in reach, followers, profile visits, and inbound conversations.

This means two things for how you produce content:

---

## 80% Fast, Low-Effort Volume Plays

Each one takes under 15 minutes. These are content asset repurposing posts (spotlights, comparisons, category roundups), quick observations, reactions to news, and build-in-public notes.

**Purpose:**
- Maintain presence on your platforms so the algorithm keeps showing your content.
- Test angles and formats at low cost. Most of these will not perform. That is expected.
- Buy enough lottery tickets for the power law to work. You cannot predict which post will hit, so you need volume.

**What "fast" means in practice:**
- Pull data from your existing content assets instead of researching from scratch. See [[engine/content-repurposing]].
- Use existing templates. See [[engine/templates]].
- Do not agonize over word choice. Ship it.
- Skip the visual if making one would push the post past 15 minutes.

**What "fast" does not mean:**
- Sloppy or careless. The post still needs one clear point and at least one specific detail.
- Repetitive. Rotate through templates and angles. Same format three days in a row trains the audience to scroll past.
- Filler. If you do not have a point for the day, skip the post. A zero-value post is worse than no post because it trains the audience to ignore you.

---

## 20% Big Swings with Real Creative Energy

One or two per week. These get the full treatment: original thinking, strong hook, visual, careful editing. This is where breakthroughs come from.

**What makes a post a "big swing":**
- It contains an original observation, not just a repurposed data point.
- The hook is crafted specifically for this post, not pulled from a template.
- It has a visual (branded card, annotated screenshot, or comparison graphic) when possible.
- It was edited at least once. The first draft was not good enough, and you spent time improving it.
- It makes a claim the reader has not seen before, or frames a familiar idea in a genuinely new way.

**When to schedule big swings:**
- Monday (both platforms) and Sunday (secondary platform) are the default big swing days. See [[engine/scheduling]].
- If a real event, product launch, or genuine insight shows up mid-week, that can become the week's big swing instead.

---

## The Trap: Equal Energy on Every Post

Spending equal energy on every post produces a flat line of mediocre content. Nothing is bad enough to ignore and nothing is good enough to share. The audience learns that your posts are predictable, which is worse than inconsistent.

Instead, be deliberately uneven. Most posts are fast and good enough. A few posts get everything you have. This unevenness is what creates the conditions for outlier performance.

---

## Volume as Lottery Tickets

You cannot predict which post will hit. The post you spent 15 minutes on might outperform the one you spent two hours crafting. The only way to increase your chances is to have enough posts in play for the power law to activate.

**Minimum volume:** Six to ten posts per week across platforms. The fast posts make this sustainable without burning out.

**Why this number:** Below six posts per week, you do not have enough surface area for the algorithm to test your content against different audiences at different times. Above ten, quality starts dropping even for fast posts, and the time investment crowds out the big swings.

---

## When Something Hits, Compound It

The single biggest mistake after a hit is treating it as a one-off and returning to the regular schedule. A hit tells you the audience cares about that specific angle. The power law rewards compounding: more content on the topic that hit, in the format that hit, at the energy level that hit.

See [[engine/hit-detection]] for the specific compounding protocol.
